Literacy Development

Building strong reading and writing skills from PP1 to Grade 4

What is Literacy?

Literacy is the foundation of learning and communication. At Jets Elementary School, we define literacy as the development of reading, writing, listening, and speaking skills that enable children to express themselves and understand the world around them.

As a key learning area in the CBC (Competency-Based Curriculum) framework, literacy helps young learners build essential skills that support their academic growth and personal development.

Why Literacy Matters

Literacy development is crucial for young learners. Here's why:

Confident Communication

Helps children express themselves clearly and communicate confidently with peers and adults.

Learning Foundation

Builds essential reading and writing skills that support learning across all subjects and grade levels.

Creative Thinking

Develops critical thinking, creativity, and imagination through storytelling and creative writing.

Self-Expression

Encourages children to express their thoughts, feelings, and ideas with confidence and clarity.

Literacy Development by Grade

Our literacy curriculum is carefully structured to meet the needs of each age group:

PP1 & PP2

Ages 4-6

  • Listening and speaking skills
  • Letter sounds (phonics)
  • Storytelling, songs, and rhymes
  • Recognizing letters and simple words

Grade 1

Ages 6-7

  • Reading simple words and sentences
  • Writing simple sentences
  • Building vocabulary
  • Basic punctuation and capitalization

Grade 2

Ages 7-8

  • Reading short stories
  • Spelling and sentence structure
  • Expressing ideas in writing
  • Reading comprehension

Grade 3 & 4

Ages 8-10

  • Reading for understanding
  • Creative writing and composition
  • Grammar and proper sentence formation
  • Presentation and public speaking skills

Our Teaching Approach

Learner-Centered & CBC-Aligned

Our literacy instruction follows the Competency-Based Curriculum (CBC) framework with a focus on:

  • Interactive Activities: Hands-on learning through games, role-play, and group work
  • Creative Expression: Encouraging storytelling, drawing, and creative writing
  • Collaborative Learning: Group discussions, peer reading, and shared writing activities
  • Differentiated Instruction: Tailoring lessons to meet individual learning needs
  • Fun & Engagement: Making literacy learning enjoyable through songs, rhymes, and picture books

We believe that when children enjoy learning, they develop a lifelong love for reading and writing.
