Creative Arts Curriculum

Introduction to Creative Arts

Creative Arts in early learning is an integrated approach that combines visual arts, music, drama, and movement to foster holistic development in young learners.

Our Creative Arts curriculum aligns with the Competency-Based Curriculum (CBC) framework, designed to nurture each child's unique talents while building essential life skills.

Aligned with CBC Curriculum Standards

Areas Covered in Creative Arts

Drawing & Painting

Exploring colors, shapes, and techniques to express ideas visually.

Music

Singing, rhythm activities, and introduction to simple instruments.

Drama

Role-play, storytelling, and simple performances to build confidence.

Craft

Hands-on activities using various materials to create 3D artworks.

Movement

Creative dance, coordination exercises, and expressive body movements.

Collaboration

Group projects that teach teamwork and appreciation of diverse perspectives.

Importance of Creative Arts

Builds Imagination & Self-expression

Creative arts provide children with a safe space to explore their thoughts, feelings, and ideas.

Encourages Teamwork & Confidence

Through group performances and collaborative projects, children learn to work together.

Develops Fine Motor Skills

Activities like cutting, drawing, and modeling strengthen hand-eye coordination.

Promotes Cultural Appreciation

Exposure to diverse art forms, music, and stories from different cultures.

Learning Outcomes by Level

Foundational Exploration (PP1 - PP2)

  • Exploring primary colors and basic shapes
  • Singing simple songs and nursery rhymes
  • Basic role-play and imaginative games
  • Free drawing with various materials
  • Simple rhythm and movement activities
  • Introduction to textures through sensory play

Skill Development (Grade 1 - 2)

  • Creative drawing with mixed media
  • Basic modeling with clay and playdough
  • Rhythm patterns and simple percussion
  • Short group performances and skits
  • Introduction to art from local culture
  • Coordinated movement and dance sequences

Creative Expression (Grade 3 - 4)

  • Introduction to design principles
  • Playing simple melodic instruments
  • Storytelling and script writing
  • Cultural dance forms and choreography
  • Art critique and appreciation sessions
  • Collaborative mural and installation projects
